Cuts

Meaning: A cut is when the skin is opened or injured by something sharp.

Burns

Meaning: A burn is damage to the skin caused by heat, fire, hot water, or chemicals.

Choking

Meaning: Choking happens when food or an object blocks the airway and makes breathing hard.

Step By Step Actions

1. Cuts

What is it? A cut is an opening in the skin caused by something sharp like glass, paper, or a knife.

How to solve the problem:
1. Wash your hands first.
2. Rinse the cut gently with clean water.
3. Press the cut with clean gauze or tissue to stop bleeding.
4. Cover it with a plaster or bandage.
5. Ask an adult or doctor for help if the cut is deep.

What we use: Clean water, gloves, tissue or gauze, antiseptic wipe, plaster, and bandage.

2. Burns

What is it? A burn is an injury to the skin caused by something hot, like boiling water, fire, or steam.

How to solve the problem:
1. Move away from the hot object safely.
2. Cool the burn under cool running water for 10 to 20 minutes.
3. Do not put ice, butter, or toothpaste on it.
4. Cover the burn with clean cloth or dressing.
5. Get medical help if the burn is serious.

What we use: Cool running water, clean dressing, clean cloth, and gloves.

3. Choking

What is it? Choking is when something blocks the throat and stops air from moving normally.

How to solve the problem:
1. Ask the person if they are choking.
2. Tell someone to call emergency services.
3. If they cannot breathe, give firm back blows between the shoulder blades.
4. If needed, use abdominal thrusts only if you are trained or an adult helps.
5. Keep checking if the object comes out.

What we use: Help from an adult, emergency phone number, and first aid knowledge.

Emergency Numbers

911

112

Tips & Safety

Always stay calm in an emergency.
Do not move an injured person unless they are in danger.

Ask an adult, teacher, or nurse for help as soon as possible.

First Aid Quiz

Answer the questions, then click the button to see the correct answers.

1. What should you do first before helping someone with a cut?

2. What should you use to cool a burn?

3. Should you put ice on a burn?

4. What should you do if someone is choking and cannot breathe?

5. Why is it important to stay calm in an emergency?

Correct Answers

1. Wash your hands first or wear gloves.

2. Use cool running water.

3. No, you should not put ice on a burn.

4. Call for help and give back blows if they cannot breathe.

5. Staying calm helps you think clearly and help the injured person faster.
